A Brief History of Silver

For thousands of years, silver has stood at the crossroads of money, power, and human ingenuity. Long before paper currencies or digital ledgers, silver was trusted as a universal store of value - shaped into coins, ornaments, and trade goods that connected civilizations across continents.

From ancient empires that minted silver as the backbone of their economies, to dramatic modern-era battles over supply, speculation, and control.

Silver's story mirrors humanity's ongoing struggle between real value and manufactured promises. Understanding this history isn't just academic - it explains why silver still matters today.
